Summary

  • LifeLink Sync stores your health data (blood type, allergies, medications, conditions) to assist in emergencies
  • Health data is classified as "special category" under GDPR Article 9 and handled with enhanced protections
  • LifeLink Sync is NOT a medical device and does NOT provide medical advice
  • You control what health data you share and can withdraw sharing consent anytime
  • Your health data accuracy is your responsibility — always keep it current

WARNING: LifeLink Sync is NOT a medical device, NOT a healthcare provider, and does NOT provide medical advice, diagnoses, or treatment recommendations. Health information stored in LifeLink Sync is for emergency reference purposes only. Always consult qualified healthcare professionals for medical decisions. In a medical emergency, call 112 (Europe), 999 (UK), or 911 (US) immediately.

1. Scope of Medical Data

What health data we collect and store:

  • Blood type
  • Known allergies (food, drug, environmental)
  • Medical conditions (chronic and acute)
  • Current medications (names entered by member)
  • Daily wellbeing scores: mood, sleep quality, pain levels, general wellbeing (via Daily Wellbeing add-on)
  • Medication schedules, dosages, and adherence logs (via Medication Reminder add-on)
  • CLARA Complete weekly wellbeing reports (AI-generated)

What we do NOT collect:

  • Detailed medical records or clinical notes
  • Laboratory test results
  • Imaging or diagnostic reports
  • Genetic or genomic data
  • Mental health treatment records (unless voluntarily entered as a medical condition)
  • Insurance or billing medical codes

2. Legal Basis for Health Data Processing (GDPR Article 9)

Health data is "special category" data under GDPR. Our legal bases:

  • Explicit consent (Art 9(2)(a)): You provide consent when entering health data and activating add-ons
  • Vital interests (Art 9(2)(c)): During emergency events, health data may be shared without additional consent to protect life
  • Substantial public interest (Art 9(2)(g)): Emergency coordination in the public interest

3. Medical Disclaimers

  • LifeLink Sync is NOT classified as a medical device under EU MDR (Medical Device Regulation) 2017/745
  • We do NOT provide clinical decision support
  • CLARA AI does NOT provide medical advice, diagnoses, or treatment recommendations
  • Wellbeing scores and reports are informational only — not clinical assessments
  • Medication reminders are notification tools — NOT prescriptions or dosage validation
  • We do NOT check for drug interactions, contraindications, or dosage safety
  • Member-entered data has NOT been verified by any healthcare professional
  • Always consult your doctor, pharmacist, or healthcare provider for medical decisions

4. Data Accuracy — Your Responsibility

You are solely responsible for the accuracy and currency of your health data.

We recommend reviewing and updating your health profile:

  • Immediately after any change in medication, allergy, or condition
  • At least every 3 months for general review
  • After any hospital visit, surgery, or significant health event

Inaccurate or outdated health data may result in:

  • Emergency responders receiving incorrect information
  • Family members receiving inaccurate wellbeing reports
  • Medication reminders that don't reflect your current prescriptions

LifeLink Sync is NOT liable for consequences arising from inaccurate member-entered data.

5. Health Data Sharing

Within your Family Circle

When you activate add-ons, you consent to sharing:

  • Daily wellbeing digests (mood, sleep, pain, wellbeing scores)
  • Medication missed notifications
  • CLARA Complete weekly reports

You can withdraw sharing consent at any time by deactivating the relevant add-on. Withdrawal stops future sharing but does not retroactively delete data already shared.

During Emergency Events

During an active SOS, the following health data may be shared under vital interests:

  • Blood type, allergies, medical conditions, current medications
  • Shared with: emergency contacts, emergency services (if integrated)

You cannot prevent emergency health data sharing during an active SOS — this is by design for your safety.

With LifeLink Sync Staff

  • Authorised administrators may access health data for customer support
  • All access is logged in our security audit trail
  • Staff receive training on health data handling

7. Security Measures for Health Data

  • AES-256 encryption at rest for all health data
  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all data in transit
  • Row-level security in database — users can only access their own health data
  • Role-based access controls for staff — principle of least privilege
  • Comprehensive audit logging of all health data access
  • Health data stored in the same secure infrastructure as all personal data (Supabase, EU Frankfurt)
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
  • Data Processing Impact Assessment (DPIA) conducted for health data processing

8. Your Rights for Health Data

Under GDPR, you have enhanced rights for special category data:

  • Access (Art 15): Request a full copy of all health data we hold
  • Rectification (Art 16): Correct any inaccurate health data via your profile or by contacting us
  • Erasure (Art 17): Request deletion of health data. Note: emergency records (7 years) cannot be erased due to legal obligations
  • Restriction (Art 18): Request we stop processing your health data while we verify accuracy
  • Portability (Art 20): Receive your health data in machine-readable format. Available in HL7 FHIR format on request for healthcare provider compatibility
  • Withdraw consent: You can withdraw consent for health data processing at any time by removing health data from your profile or deactivating add-ons
  • Object (Art 21): Object to processing of your health data

Contact medical-privacy@lifelink-sync.com to exercise any right. Response within 30 days.

9. Emergency Override

  • In situations where we reasonably believe there is a risk to life, we may share health data without additional consent
  • This is permitted under GDPR Article 9(2)(c) — vital interests
  • Emergency override applies only during active SOS events or when credible risk to life is identified
  • Emergency overrides are logged and auditable
  • You will be informed of any emergency data sharing after the event
