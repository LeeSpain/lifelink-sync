Data Processing Agreement

Effective Date: March 2026 | Last Updated: March 2026 | Next Review: September 2026

This document is also available in Spanish and Dutch.

Summary

  • This agreement explains how LifeLink Sync processes your personal data as a data controller under GDPR.
  • We use trusted sub-processors (Supabase, Stripe, Resend, Twilio, Google Analytics, Sentry) to operate our platform.
  • All sub-processors have data processing agreements in place and meet EU data protection standards.
  • Your data is primarily processed within the EU/EEA, with US transfers covered by Standard Contractual Clauses.
  • We conduct regular audits of our sub-processors to ensure ongoing compliance.

1. Definitions

  • Controller: LifeLink Sync (the entity determining purposes and means of processing).
  • Data Subject: You, the member using LifeLink Sync.
  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable person.
  • Special Category Data: Health data, biometric data, and other categories per GDPR Article 9.
  • Processing: Any operation performed on personal data.
  • Sub-processor: Third-party service provider processing data on our behalf.
  • Supervisory Authority: AEPD (Spain), ICO (UK), or relevant local authority.

2. Role of LifeLink Sync

  • LifeLink Sync acts as the Data Controller for all personal data collected through the platform.
  • We determine the purposes and means of processing.
  • We are responsible for ensuring compliance with GDPR and applicable data protection laws.
  • Our registered office is in Madrid, Spain.

5. Data Subject Rights

We will assist you in exercising your GDPR rights:

  • Access — requests responded to within 30 days.
  • Rectification — processed promptly.
  • Erasure — completed where legally permissible (emergency/financial records excluded per legal retention).
  • Restriction — applied where requested during verification.
  • Portability — provided in JSON or CSV format; health data in HL7 FHIR on request.
  • Objection — reviewed within 30 days.

All rights requests: privacy@lifelink-sync.com

6. Security Obligations

  • AES-256 encryption at rest
  • TLS 1.3 encryption in transit
  • Row-level security (RLS) in database
  • Role-based access controls (RBAC)
  • Multi-factor authentication for staff access
  • Regular vulnerability scanning and penetration testing
  • Security incident response plan maintained and tested
  • Employee security awareness training
  • Physical security managed by sub-processors (SOC 2 compliance)

7. Data Breach Response

  • Detection: Continuous monitoring via security audit logs and auth failure tracking.
  • Assessment: Within 24 hours of detection, assess scope, impact, and affected data subjects.
  • Authority notification: Within 72 hours of becoming aware, notify relevant supervisory authority (AEPD/ICO) per GDPR Article 33.
  • Individual notification: Without undue delay where breach is likely to result in high risk to rights and freedoms per GDPR Article 34.
  • Documentation: All breaches documented in breach register per GDPR Article 33(5).
  • Remediation: Root cause analysis and corrective measures implemented.

Contact for breach reporting: dpo@lifelink-sync.com

9. International Data Transfers

  • Primary processing: EU/EEA (Supabase Frankfurt).
  • UK transfers: Permitted under UK adequacy decision (28 June 2021, extended).
  • US transfers: Covered by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) adopted under Commission Decision 2021/914.
  • Emergency transfers: Permitted under GDPR Article 49(1)(e) — vital interests.
  • We do not transfer data to countries without adequate protection unless covered by SCCs or other approved mechanisms.
  • Transfer impact assessments conducted for all US sub-processors.

10. Audit Rights

  • You may request information about our data processing practices.
  • We will provide evidence of compliance upon reasonable request.
  • We engage independent auditors for annual compliance reviews.
  • Audit results summaries available upon request to dpo@lifelink-sync.com.

11. Data Return and Deletion

On account termination:

  • You can request a complete data export before deletion.
  • Data export available in JSON format; health data in HL7 FHIR on request.
  • After export or upon request, personal data deleted within 30 days.
  • Exceptions: data retained under legal obligation (emergency records 7 years, financial records 7 years).
  • Anonymised analytics data may be retained indefinitely.
  • Backups purged within 90 days of deletion request.

12. Governing Law

  • This DPA is governed by the laws of Spain, in accordance with GDPR.
  • For UK data subjects, UK Data Protection Act 2018 applies.
  • Disputes subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of Madrid, Spain.
  • Without prejudice to the right to lodge complaints with supervisory authorities.
