1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device to store information. They help websites remember your preferences, keep you logged in, and understand how you use the service. In addition to traditional cookies, modern web applications also use similar technologies such as localStorage (data stored persistently in your browser), sessionStorage (data stored only for the duration of your browser session), and service workers (scripts that enable offline functionality and caching). This policy covers all of these technologies as used by LifeLink Sync.

4. Service Worker and PWA Caching

LifeLink Sync is a Progressive Web App (PWA) that uses a service worker to provide offline functionality and improved performance:

  • We use a service worker for Progressive Web App (PWA) functionality
  • Cached content includes: application code, stylesheets, images
  • Supabase API responses are cached with a NetworkFirst strategy (60-minute expiration)
  • Image uploads are cached with a CacheFirst strategy (90-day expiration)
  • Cache is automatically managed and updated when new versions are deployed
  • You can clear the cache by uninstalling the PWA or clearing browser data

6. How to Manage Cookies

Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Instructions vary by browser:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

Google Analytics opt-out: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

Impact of blocking cookies: Blocking essential cookies will prevent you from logging in to LifeLink Sync. Blocking analytics cookies will not affect your use of the service.

Mobile devices: Similar controls are available in your device's browser settings.

7. Do Not Track

We respect Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals. When DNT is enabled, analytics cookies will not be set. Your use of LifeLink Sync will not be affected in any other way.
