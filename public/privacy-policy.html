5. CLARA AI and Automated Processing

CLARA is our AI assistant that helps with emergency protection, wellbeing check-ins, and medication reminders. This section explains how CLARA processes your data.

What CLARA Processes

  • Your profile data (name, age, medical information)
  • Conversation messages you send to CLARA
  • Health and wellbeing data (when the Daily Wellbeing or Medication Reminder add-ons are active)

How CLARA Uses Your Data

  • Conversation memory is stored for up to 90 days to provide relevant context in future conversations
  • CLARA learns from interactions to improve responses, stored in a knowledge base
  • Vector embeddings are created from your data to power search and retrieval functionality

Your Rights Regarding CLARA

  • GDPR Article 22: CLARA does not make decisions that produce legal effects or similarly significantly affect you. All AI outputs are informational only.
  • You have the right to request human review of any CLARA output at any time
  • You can opt out of CLARA processing by not using the chat feature and deactivating any active add-ons
Important: CLARA Complete reports are AI-generated and are not reviewed by clinical professionals. They are intended for informational purposes only and should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice.

Your data is not used to train external AI models.

For complete details about how CLARA processes your data, please see our AI Data Policy.

7. Your Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject, you have the following rights under the GDPR. We are committed to making it straightforward for you to exercise them.

Right of Access (Article 15)

You have the right to request a copy of all personal data we hold about you. We will provide this in a commonly used electronic format within 30 days.

Right to Rectification (Article 16)

You have the right to correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data. You can update most information directly via your profile settings, or contact us for assistance.

Right to Erasure (Article 17)

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data. Please note that certain records cannot be erased due to legal obligations:

  • Emergency / SOS records: retained for 7 years
  • Financial / billing records: retained for 7 years

Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)

You have the right to request that we limit how we process your data — for example, while we verify the accuracy of data you have disputed.

Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format. Health data is available in HL7 FHIR format on request.

Right to Object (Article 21)

You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. Where you object to marketing, we will stop processing immediately.

Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

CLARA does not make automated decisions that produce legal effects or similarly significantly affect you. You can request human review of any AI-generated output at any time.

Right to Withdraw Consent

You may withdraw any consent you have given at any time, including consent for family circle sharing, CLARA AI processing, and marketing communications. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

How to Exercise Your Rights

Email privacy@lifelink-sync.com with your request. We will respond within 30 days. There is no fee for exercising your rights, unless requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive.

We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request, to protect your data from unauthorised access.

Supervisory Authorities

If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority:

  • Spain: Agencia Española de Protección de Datos (AEPD) — www.aepd.es
  • UK: Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) — ico.org.uk
  • Or your local EU/EEA supervisory authority

10. International Data Transfers

  • Primary processing: All primary data processing takes place within the EU/EEA (Supabase, Frankfurt, Germany).
  • UK transfers: Permitted under the UK adequacy decision.
  • US transfers: Transfers to Stripe, Resend, Twilio, Google, and Sentry are covered by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs).
  • Emergency transfers: In an active emergency, data transfers may occur under the vital interests basis regardless of the recipient's location.
  • We do not transfer data to countries without adequate protection except under SCCs or other approved safeguards (e.g., binding corporate rules, adequacy decisions).

11. Security

We implement robust technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data:

  • Encryption at rest: AES-256 encryption for all stored data
  • Encryption in transit: TLS 1.3 for all data transmissions
  • Row-level security: Database-level controls ensuring users can only access their own data
  • Role-based access: Staff access is restricted by role, following the principle of least privilege
  • Security audits: Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Authentication monitoring: Automated monitoring of authentication failures with rate limiting to prevent brute-force attacks
  • Password security: Passwords are securely hashed and never stored in plain text

12. Data Breach Notification

  • We will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach, as required by GDPR Article 33.
  • We will notify affected individuals without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms, as required by GDPR Article 34.
  • We maintain a breach register as required by GDPR Article 33(5), documenting all breaches, their effects, and remedial actions taken.
